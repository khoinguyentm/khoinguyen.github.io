Original Product

"Basketball Blueprint" is an assistant tool for basketball coaches, the app has a total of 135+ drill, on-court demonstration videos, clipboard, practice builder, and 41+ strategies in playbook included.

But here are the problems: the app looks outdated, very limited access on free version, navigation is puzzling, and you actually have to pay $28.99 just for the clipboard. If you decide to keep the free trial, you have to pay $4.99 to unlock each additional drills and practice ideas.

Research

Research Challenge

I encountered a challenging moment in the begining of my research: finding a specific amount of people with insight of coaching. I needed to target coaches that use mobile apps to support their coaching career and ask about how they feel about “Basketball Blueprint”, and expanded my research to any experience of coaching and users who played sports at any level.

User Persona

When I created my first persona, I realised that persona was a highly experienced high school basketball coach. I decided to focus on Mitchell, he used to play in NJB (National Junior Basketball) and now he's currently coaching a NJB team.

What Mitchell needs is an accessible app for quick and effective coaching ideas, and he wants to level up his coaching experience to coach better.

Interview Results For "Basketball Blueprint"

The results came back to me and I did not surprise. 100% of people said that the app looks outdated, they didn't understand what "Blueprint" means in sports like basketball, logo doesn't make sense, icons are not eye-catching, hard to navigate, nobody wanted to pay $28.99 for just unlock the clipboard, and very limited access for free trial.

Definition & Ideation

Competitor Analysis

During my competitor analysis, I discovered that "CoachBase" is a redesign brand of "Basketball coach's clipboard". Their product became a powerful tool and it designed by coaches for coaches. The app features high quality drill videos, smart and easy to understand clipboard animations, intuitive design, automatic timer, and the app is world wide. On the other hand, "Youtube" and other experienced coaches are my indirect competitors. During my research I learned that people go to "Youtube" to learn and research plays or drills, and they would find some experienced coaches to ask for advices.

Affinity Diagram

From my collected data from user interviews, I created 2 affinity diagrams to organize and generate ideas. These 2 affinity diagram (below) shows my findings during my user interviews. I interviewed a total 4 people that have coaching experience on different levels or interest in coaching. I discovered that none of them are using coaching app or don’t like to use it because of many reasons: never heard of the app, upgrade plan is expensive not worth it, hidden upgrade features, and it’s frustrated to use.

From there I made I Like, I Wish, What If and learned that if users would download and use the app, they would like to share their drills with other coaches, a brand new interface, and a full access for trial base for a specific amount of time.
