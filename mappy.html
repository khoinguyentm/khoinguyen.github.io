MappyHour

  • Timeline: 3 weeks
  • My Role: UI/UX designer
  • Tools: Adobe XD, Invision

Overall Concept

What is MappyHour?

MappyHour is a mobile application to help adults hoping to save money when going out to eat and/or drink to solve the issue of finding affordable restaurants and bars. We’re finding you new happy hour deals every day. Even we use our app daily.

Problem & Solution

There are currently very few apps that allow users to search for happy hours at nearby restaurants. These apps have very few resources for users to search for happy hour in surrounding areas and very frustration when using. How do we fix this? Me and 2 other designers had decided to create an app to give users the flexibility to find happy hour deals nearby by sharing their location and allowing them to filter their search such as times, cuisines, prices, etc.

Design Process: Research

Interview & Survey

Through user interviews and survey methods we gathered insights from a 28 men and women between the ages of 21 and 45 years old to learn more about general social habits, opinions, and behaviors.

User Insight

During the team's interview, we discovered that food and drinks are priorities for socializing. People also like to spend time with family or their group of friends at their regular spots. But they’re still open for suggestion to visit new places. Therefore, we believed that special deals, or happy hour for food and drinks will make outings more exciting and make the gathering more enjoyable for people in their social experience, and that we might be able to help if we create an app that can locate special deals and happy hour in their areas and inform them the hours at the best convenient time. We might do this by letting users use their location while searching for happy hour/special deals and then show them a list of results so they can make a selection. Doing this will allow our product to save money for anyone and spend time with their friends/family.

Affinity Diagram

After our interviews, we gathered and analyzed answers. From there we created an affinity diagram (above). This diagram helped us to understand that users would find what are the special deals, who has these offers, times of happy hour, and what's in happy hour menu.

Empathy Map

Our next step in this project was to create an empathy map (below). This empathy map had shown us the pains and the gains of our users. Our findings:

    Pains:

  • Picky eaters & dietary restrictions.
  • Work conflicts with happy hour times.
  • Times & pricing = main considerations.

    Gains:

  • Spending time with friends and family.
  • Being a regular to get to know people and places.

User Persona

Our main focus in this project was Tom Smith. Tom is 26 years old, he's currently working as a flooring salesman. He needs to search for special deals at restaurants or places that offer happy hour because he wants to be social but stay within his budget.

Ideation Process

I like, I wish, What if… & Feature Prioritization Matrix

We used the I like, I wish, What if… method and feature prioritization matrix for brainstorming and ideating the key features of our app. We decided that our app should include these key features:

  • What time to go out?
  • Being able to see the menu beforehand.
  • Restaurant traffic and the busiest times.
  • Finding the best deals near me.
  • Restaurants’ ability to promote or advertise new deals to users.

User Journey Map

After creating user journey, we found a few opportunities for app improvement. Task: to create an account with email and browse happy hour deals nearby.

    Opportunities for improvement:

  • Include onboarding and an option to either create an account or continue as a guest.
  • Option to browse nearby without actually starting a search.
  • Deal-of-the-day restaurants that offer extra points for checking in or making reservations through the app (opportunity for ROI).
  • Indicate parking options at the location (valet, parking garage, etc.)

Competitor Analysis

For our competitor, we discovered that "The House" maps out happy hour deals in surrounding areas, detail about happy hour time and prices, and lots of choices between food and drinks. The app is easy to use but usually crash/freeze while using, has no real value offers, and only provides five different locations in United States.

Prototyping & Testing

For this prototype users will create an account by email, browse the homepage, discover information about Fat House, and finally schedule a uber to Fat House.

Iterations From Testing

  • Bring the favorites page out of the hamburger menu.
  • Shrink the logo.
  • Combine search and filter together
  • Shrink hamburger menu icon
  • Provide option to view search results on a map
  • Added a time chart to visually display the busiest times at a restaurant each day.
  • Added the ability to go to the Uber or Lyft apps to call a ride to the restaurant address.

Future Thinking & Developing

For future thinking of improvement, the app could provide both users and businesses to utilize the app by offering exclusive deals to users from restaurants in their area and marketing opportunities for restaurants to advertise to users on the app. Also, expand more search results and give discount on Uber/Lyft rides for those who continually use the app.
