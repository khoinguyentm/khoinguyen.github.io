Responsive Redesign: Paws 4 Healing

  • Timeline: 4 weeks
  • My Role: UI/UX designer
  • Tools: Adobe CC, Invision

Current Website

What is Paws 4 Healing?

Paws 4 Healing is an all volunteer, 501(c)(3) non-profit organization in southern California. Their volunteer teams provide animal-assisted activity and animal-assisted therapy services for healthcare facilities, schools, and other kinds of social agencies.

Problem & Solution

Problem Statement

The original website has an outdated design that takes away from the credibility of the Paws 4 Healing. My team believe redesigning the "paws 4 healing" website to be more accessible, flexible, and credible for volunteers, members and general public will achieve increase engagement with the community and donations for the cause

Goal

The goal for this project was recreate Paws 4 Healing’s branding and introduce simple user flows with modern design, also create responsive for smart devices.

Research

Survey Results

During my research phase with two other designers we did in-person interviews, survey, and eventually we got a hold of the stakeholder. Base on the survey, our participants are mostly mid career working professional and they don't have a lot of time for volunteer but they do have a lot of resources to donate. After collecting the results, we analyzed the data using multiple techniques.

Affinity Diagram

From our in-person interview results. We analyzed the answers and information from our participants and created a affinity diagram (below). The affinity diagram shed keys insights into our research findings and allowed us to easily extract valuable information.

User Persona

The affinity diagram illustrated above helped create the user persona (below) for this project. The user persona was a helpful tools throughout the design process and bridge the gap between research and planning. In this project we focused on Susan, a college student.

Susan's Journey Map

The user journey displays a typical process that involves scheduling a pet therapy event for Susan's school. We created the map to empathize with Susan and to showcase Susan's emotions, thoughts and actions during her scheduling process. We ultimately extracted key points, Susan's needs and pain points.

Definition & Ideation

Competitor Analysis

While we were working on our analysis, we thought that "Pet Partners" were going to be our main competitor but when we talked to the stakeholder turned out they are partners and every action is through "Pet Partners". People contact "Pet Partners" and they transfer them to "Paws 4 Healing" based on their location. "Love 4 Paws" and "UCI Health" are our competitors, specify in having people volunteer to be handlers and bringing their animals but not so much for scheduling a therapy.

SWOT

This SWOT analysis (below) on original website we found that they did have their own contents they were very explicit about becoming a pet handlers and the things that they do. Even with the outdated look their navigation structure was decent and everything was layout simple.

Style Guide

Below is the style guide that I created. The colors look more vibrant, fun and happy. We are using SF Pro Display and Roboto for typography to make the website looks more clean and modern. We also created button interactions to let user know when the button is disabled and when is active.

Logo Redesign

During my logo redesign process, I broke down the meaning of their brand. The reason why I chose pink over their yellow is because they have a very tight relationship with "Pet Partners" so I designed "Paws 4 Healing" looks relative with their partner and then I started to add more illustrations to make the logo has more meaning.

Prototyping & Testing

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Through out research and sketching, I landed at the first low fidelity wireframes. The task for this wireframe is schedule a pet therapy event at a school. Based on desired event location and time, Susan can easily schedule her event at her school through simple and straight forward scheduling process.

High Fidelity Versions

Main Takeaway From Testing

  • Allow users to mark their favorite animals.
  • Design a pending landing page so users can bookmark.
  • Pop-up warning when go back while filling forms.
  • Stay with color palette.
  • Footer needs improve.
  • Take out the shadow behind logo since the website is flat.

At this point, I am eager to jump into my next project. I delivered a consistent, clean, modern look product that solves a real problem for Susan and everyone struggling with the original website.
